Latest news + more

  • Gastropub distillery the Three-Broomsticks now farm-to-table, Madam Rosmerta says

    Gastropub distillery the Three Broomsticks is now farm-to-table, proprietor Madame Rosmeta says. Popular with students and townsfolk alike, the venue has capitlaized on the latest trend of fresh food straight from teh fields and greenhouses. Rosmerta states that though most supplies are local to Scotland, certain foods are being Apparated in each morning.

  • Squid in Hogwarts Lake found hosting a party with cronuts for mer-people

    In the bizarre moment of the week, the Hogwarts Lake Squid was caught having a raucous party with the local merpeople the other night, complete with the new pastry craze, the cronut. Deputy Headmistress McGonagall has not ruled out student pranking by a certain pair of red-headed twins, who were proven to be the culprits behind the jackelope invasion of the town fountain last year.

  • Organic raw sweets now available at Honeydukes, overpriced

    Popular local sweet shop Honeydukes, known for delectable treats, is now offering somethign a little healthier: organic, raw sweets. Some locals are less than thrilled, including Madam Spinnet, who griped, "it's just another over-priced ploy to get our business!" However, school nurse Madam Pomfrey is said to be overjoyed that the schoolchildren will have healthier options.

  • Socialite and part-veela Poppy Warbeck engaged to Quidditch Star

    In the latest from celebrity gossip in London, Poppy Warbeck, the sister of popular singer Celestina Warbeck, is reportedly engaged to Irish National Team seeker, Aidan Lynch. Famously beautiful due to her Veela heritage, Poppy Warbeck is a style icon whose wedding dress is expected to cost millions of Galleons. She was previously linked to Bulgarian seeker Viktor Krum.
